You have what it takes to be a victorious, independent, fearless Woman

As a regular contributor to an Australian teen magazine, I tend to be of the opinion that teenage girls - and the publications they read - are smarter than they're usually given credit for. Nonetheless, there are some things that any publication that relies on advertising from the fashion and beauty industries (as all girls' and women's magazines do) can't publish if it wants to stick around for long. That's where Kaz Cooke's latest offering, Girl Stuff: Your Full-on Guide to the Teen Years, comes in. Born from a survey of more than 4000 girls around Australia (whose remarks are scattered throughout the book), Girl Stuff is a kind of teen magazine omnibus that even the most progressive parents would feel happy to hand to their daughter (socially conservative parents are another matter - Cooke covers sex and drugs more candidly than most health classes.
